Who I am

01 About

I completed my Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering (Communication Theory & Systems) at UC San Diego, advised by Prof. Nuria González-Prelcic in the WiSeCom Lab. My dissertation, "Hybrid Model/Data-Driven Solutions in ISAC: Improving Link Establishment, Channel Estimation, and User Localization in mmWave Vehicular Networks," develops physics-informed deep learning methods for integrated sensing and communication, spanning passive-radar-aided mmWave link configuration, single-snapshot 3D vehicle localization, and continuous channel/position/orientation tracking for V2I and V2V systems. I began the program at NC State University and transferred to UCSD with my advisor in 2024.

Before my Ph.D., I received an M.S. in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Texas at Austin, working in the Wireless Systems Innovation Laboratory (WSIL) with Prof. Nuria González-Prelcic on UAV navigation and deep reinforcement learning.

Across my M.S. and Ph.D. years I completed two research internships at Ericsson and three engineering internships at Qualcomm, where I'm now a Senior Engineer in San Diego.
